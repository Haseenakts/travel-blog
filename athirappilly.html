Athirappilly

The Niagara of India

About Athirappilly

Athirappilly, located in Thrissur district, is home to Kerala's largest waterfall. Cascading from a height of nearly 80 feet, the Athirappilly Waterfalls are surrounded by lush evergreen forests and the Chalakudy River. The breathtaking scenery and refreshing atmosphere make it one of Kerala's most popular tourist destinations.

Athirappilly has also been featured in many Indian movies, making it a favorite destination for photographers, filmmakers, families, and nature lovers.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Athirappilly is from September to January, when the waterfall is full after the monsoon and the weather is cool and pleasant. Monsoon season offers spectacular views but can be slippery, so visitors should take extra care.

Top Attractions

  • Athirappilly Waterfalls
  • Vazhachal Waterfalls
  • Charpa Waterfalls
  • Sholayar Dam

  • Rainforest Trekking
  • Butterfly Garden
  • Scenic Viewpoints

Things to Do

  • Enjoy the spectacular waterfall views.
  • Take nature walks through the rainforest.
  • Visit nearby Vazhachal and Charpa waterfalls.
  • Capture stunning landscape photographs.
  • Watch exotic birds and butterflies.
  • Enjoy a peaceful family picnic surrounded by nature.

Must-Try Foods

  • Kerala Fish Curry
  • Kappa and Meen Curry
  • Appam with Chicken Stew
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Kerala Sadya
  • Fresh Tender Coconut Water

Travel Tips

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes.
  • Carry an umbrella or raincoat during the monsoon.
  • Do not cross safety barriers near the waterfall.
  • Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds.
  • Keep the surroundings clean and avoid plastic waste.
