Thekkady

Home of the Famous Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

About Thekkady

Thekkady, located in the Idukki district of Kerala, is one of the state's most popular eco-tourism destinations. Surrounded by the lush forests of the Western Ghats, it is best known for the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, where visitors can spot elephants, deer, wild boar, bison, and a wide variety of birds in their natural habitat.

Besides wildlife, Thekkady is famous for its scenic landscapes, spice plantations, bamboo rafting, trekking trails, and peaceful boating experiences on Periyar Lake.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Thekkady is from October to February, when the climate is cool and comfortable for wildlife safaris, trekking, and sightseeing. Monsoon season also offers lush greenery and beautiful landscapes.

Top Attractions

  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Periyar Lake
  • Mangala Devi Temple
  • Mullaperiyar Dam
  • Spice Plantations
  • Elephant Junction
  • Chellarkovil Viewpoint
  • Abraham's Spice Garden

Things to Do

  • Take a boat safari on Periyar Lake.
  • Enjoy a guided jungle trek.
  • Visit aromatic spice plantations.
  • Experience bamboo rafting.
  • Watch traditional Kathakali and Kalaripayattu performances.
  • Go birdwatching and wildlife photography.

Must-Try Foods

  • Kerala Sadya
  • Appam with Vegetable or Chicken Stew
  • Kappa with Fish Curry
  • Spicy Kerala Beef Fry
  • Cardamom Tea
  • Homemade Chocolates and Fresh Spices

Travel Tips

  • Book boat safari tickets early, especially during holidays.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and trekking shoes.
  • Carry binoculars for wildlife and birdwatching.
  • Maintain silence inside the wildlife sanctuary.
  • Do not feed or disturb wild animals.
  • Purchase authentic spices from certified local shops.
