Wayanad

Nature's Paradise in the Western Ghats

About Wayanad

Wayanad is one of Kerala's most scenic hill districts, nestled in the Western Ghats. Known for its lush forests, rolling hills, waterfalls, wildlife sanctuaries, tea and coffee plantations, and ancient caves, Wayanad is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Whether you enjoy trekking, wildlife photography, camping, or simply relaxing amidst nature, Wayanad offers unforgettable experiences throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Wayanad is from October to May, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon season (June to September) transforms the region into a lush green paradise, making it especially beautiful for waterfall lovers.

Top Attractions

  • Edakkal Caves
  • Soochipara Waterfalls
  • Banasura Sagar Dam
  • Chembra Peak
  • Pookode Lake
  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Kuruvadweep Island

Things to Do

  • Trek to Chembra Peak.
  • Visit the ancient Edakkal Caves.
  • Enjoy boating at Pookode Lake.
  • Take a jeep safari through wildlife sanctuaries.
  • Explore tea, coffee, and spice plantations.
  • Camp under the stars in the hills.

Must-Try Foods

  • Malabar Biryani
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Appam with Vegetable Stew
  • Bamboo Rice Payasam
  • Kerala Fish Curry
  • Fresh Wayanad Coffee

Travel Tips

  • Carry light woollen clothes during winter.
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes.
  • Book trekking permits in advance where required.
  • Carry rain gear if visiting during the monsoon.
  • Respect wildlife and avoid littering.
