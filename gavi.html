Gavi

Kerala's Untouched Eco-Tourism Paradise

About Gavi

Gavi is a serene eco-tourism destination nestled within the Periyar Tiger Reserve in Pathanamthitta district, Kerala. Surrounded by evergreen forests, rolling hills, tranquil lakes, and rich wildlife, Gavi is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Managed by the Kerala Forest Development Corporation, Gavi is known for its sustainable tourism, offering visitors an opportunity to experience the beauty of untouched forests while promoting wildlife conservation.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Gavi is from September to February, when the weather is cool, pleasant, and perfect for trekking, boating, and wildlife spotting.

Top Attractions

  • Gavi Lake
  • Periyar Tiger Reserve
  • Sabarimala View Point
  • Grassland Trekking Trails
  • Cardamom Plantations
  • Eco-Lodge
  • Forest Camping
  • Scenic Viewpoints

Things to Do

  • Enjoy a guided jungle safari.
  • Go boating on the peaceful Gavi Lake.
  • Take part in nature walks and trekking.
  • Watch birds and wildlife in their natural habitat.
  • Visit spice and cardamom plantations.
  • Stay overnight in eco-lodges or forest camps.

Must-Try Foods

  • Kerala Sadya
  • Kappa with Fish Curry
  • Appam with Vegetable Stew
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry
  • Fresh Cardamom Tea
  • Locally Grown Spices and Honey

Travel Tips

  • Carry a valid ID for forest entry.
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes and full-sleeved clothing.
  • Carry binoculars and a camera for birdwatching.
  • Follow forest department rules and avoid disturbing wildlife.
  • Avoid plastic and help preserve the natural environment.
  • Book eco-tourism activities in advance during weekends and holidays.
